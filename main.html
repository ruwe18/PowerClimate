Do you know the consequences of Carbon Dioxides emissions in the world?

  • Heatwaves in Rio Grande do Sul: A Climate Crisis Unfolds

    Extreme heatwaves, driven by rising CO₂ emissions, have plagued Brazil’s southernmost state. Agricultural lands are drying up, leaving farmers in crisis, while cities struggle with record temperatures.

  • Tuvalu's Struggle Against Rising Seas

    Tuvalu, a small island nation, is being submerged due to rising sea levels accelerated by global CO₂ emissions. Communities face displacement as their homes vanish, raising urgent questions about climate migration.

  • Australia's Wildfire Crisis: A Carbon-Fueled Disaster

    Rampant wildfires have devastated Australia, with CO₂ emissions contributing to longer, more intense fire seasons, leading to widespread destruction and ecological loss.

  • The Philippines: Typhoon Haiyan and the Impact of Climate Change Emissions

    Typhoon Haiyan, intensified by rising CO₂ emissions, struck the Philippines in November 2013, causing thousands of deaths and widespread destruction. Urgent climate action is needed to reduce emissions and protect vulnerable communities from future disasters.

With all the disasters occuring, our team, Power Climate developed a solution to raise awareness about the climate crisis and the consequences of it.

Buying products from big companies often feels convenient, but it fuels a cycle of mass production that drives up CO2 emissions. The manufacturing, shipping, and packaging processes behind these goods rely heavily on fossil fuels, contributing to climate disasters like stronger storms and rising sea levels. Our consumption today is accelerating tomorrow’s environmental collapse.

About us

The Power Climate project was conceived and developed by six high school students from ORT School. Research, visual identity creation, and presentation were carried out by Davi Lemos and Theo Mortari. The main webpage, which informs users about the climate crisis and offers suggestions for action, was developed by Daniel Vergaça and Júlia Peixoto. Finally, Francisco Carvalho and Guilherme Holtz manipulated technical data from the US GHG Center to create an interactive map that shows emission levels in different locations and time periods.



Our Objective

We aim to reach the general public and provide information about the global environmental situation in a clear and objective manner. With greater awareness of the issue, we can work towards a more balanced and healthy world.
